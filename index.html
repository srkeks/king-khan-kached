Curatorial Note

This is a born-digital archive that uses Shah Rukh Khan (srk) to look at the early web. Srk was the most-uploaded face of the era making him an appropriate probe to measure the web's distortions.

His rise and the web's rise followed the same arc and as Srk became the face of Hindi cinema through the 1990s, the internet was reaching India and the South Asian diaspora that loved him — students, families, workers scattered across South Asia, the Gulf, Britain, North America and beyond.

Srk was himself an early believer in the web. In November 2000 he launched srkworld.com, an entertainment portal backed with a million dollars and partners including Juhi Chawla, with B4U supplying broadband content. Within a year it was gone, closed as his production company's losses mounted and the dotcom bubble burst, undone by the absence of a workable revenue model.

As the curator of this collection I have my own motivations, twenty-five years on this planet and twenty-five on planet SRK. I was there in the late-2000s googling SRK, collecting countless DVDs of his movies, and watching Om Shanti Om until I lost count. What decayed online is also decaying in me; the internet archive and my memory seem to be collapsing at the same rate.
